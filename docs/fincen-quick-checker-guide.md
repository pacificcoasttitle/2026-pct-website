## Common Exemptions (Even If the 3 Triggers Apply)

Even if a transaction clears all three questions as "reportable," certain transfers may still be **exempt**. The file should always document the basis for any exemption.

- Easement transfers
- Transfers due to death, divorce, or dissolution
- Transfers to a bankruptcy estate
- Court-supervised transfers
- No-consideration transfers by an individual (alone or with spouse) to a trust where that individual/spouse is the settlor or grantor
- 1031 exchange transfers to a qualified intermediary (note: the sale out of the exchange may still be reportable depending on facts)

**Some entities and trusts may also fall into exemption categories:**
- Certain regulated financial institutions and registered entities
- Governmental authorities
- Certain securities reporting issuers and entities wholly owned by them

*Exemptions can be technical. Always confirm with escrow and counsel.*
