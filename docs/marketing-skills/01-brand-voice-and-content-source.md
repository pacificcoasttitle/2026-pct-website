## Section 3: Audience Language

PCT speaks to at least five distinct audiences on the public site. The vocabulary shifts noticeably between them.

### 3.1 Real estate agents
- **Preferred term:** "agents" or "real estate professionals" — used throughout `/resources` and `/fincen/agents`. "REALTORS®" was not observed in scanned pages.
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer and operational. PCT treats the agent as the ally who can shape client expectations, not as someone to be sold to.
- **Sample agent-directed phrasing:** "You're not the filer—but you can prevent delays with one move" [^fincen-agents]; "Set expectations early"; "Send us the scenario early."
- **Benefits PCT emphasizes for agents:** Speed ("close deals faster" [^resources]), prospecting power (PCT Property Pro), training and reference at hand ("Information at your fingertips" [^flyers]).
- **CTAs to agents:** "Talk to an Escrow Officer," "Send us the scenario early," "Check if it's reportable" [^fincen-agents].

### 3.2 Lenders / loan officers
- **Preferred term:** "lenders" — addressed in the commercial, nationwide, credit-union, and TSG/REO pages.
- **Tone:** Transactional and capability-focused. Volume, multi-state coverage, and operational reliability are the recurring promises.
- **Sample lender-directed phrasing:** "Lenders with nationwide loan portfolios" [^nationwide]; "Competitive pricing designed for credit union volume" [^services-credit-unions].
- **Benefits PCT emphasizes for lenders:** Single point of contact, regulatory compliance support, dedicated account managers, "priority processing" [^services-credit-unions].

### 3.3 Escrow officers, trustees, attorneys (partner professionals)
- **Preferred term:** "trustees, attorneys, lenders, and servicers" [^services-tsg].
- **Tone:** Specialist-to-specialist. Jargon is used without explanation (BOI, AML, beneficial owner, trustee sales) [^fincen].
- **Sample phrasing:** "Title guarantees issued at foreclosure initiation to ensure compliance and manage risk" [^services-tsg].

### 3.4 Consumers / homeowners / homebuyers
- **Preferred term:** "homebuyers," "homeowners," and direct second-person "you."
- **Tone:** Reassuring and explanatory. Jargon is introduced, then translated.
- **Sample consumer-directed phrasing:** "Get answers to your title and escrow questions right now—not tomorrow" [^homepage variant — see §11 note]; "Plain-English explanations at signing" [^services-escrow]; "Education at your fingertips" [^booklets].
- **Benefits PCT emphasizes for consumers:** Protection, clarity, single dedicated officer, lifetime coverage.

### 3.5 PCT's own sales reps
**Not addressed on the public site.** Any voice/guidance specific to sales-rep-facing content will need to come from internal sources — flagged in §10.

### 3.6 Where the tone shifts most sharply
- **Most casual:** Escrow page ("You get the keys. The seller gets paid. Everyone's happy.") [^services-escrow] — for consumers.
- **Most technical:** TSG/REO page — assumes the reader knows what a trustee sale guarantee is [^services-tsg].
- **Most peer-to-peer:** FinCEN/Agents page — talks to the agent as a co-operator, not a client [^fincen-agents].

## Section 4: Voice & Tone Patterns

### 4.1 Pronoun usage
- **"We" dominates on service pages.** On `/services/escrow`, "we" appears 50+ times vs. "Pacific Coast Title" used mainly in headers and footer [^services-escrow]. The pattern reverses on the homepage, where the branded name appears ~15+ times vs. "we" ~6 times [^homepage].
- **Pattern:** Branded name for first reference and identity moments; "we" for service delivery and reassurance.

### 4.2 Direct address ("you")
- **Heavy throughout.** "Protect What's Yours" [^services-title]; "Find the office nearest you" [^locations]; "Your closing is in experienced hands" [^services-escrow]; "Education at your fingertips" [^booklets].
- The reader is consistently the subject of the sentence.

### 4.3 Contractions
- **Used freely.** "We're," "don't," "it's," "you're," "everyone's" — appear across the About page, escrow page, and FinCEN-for-agents page [^about] [^services-escrow] [^fincen-agents].
- This is one of the strongest signals that PCT's public voice is **conversational-professional**, not formal-legal.

### 4.4 Sentence length and rhythm
- **Mix of short punch and medium structure.** The escrow page closes a section with three short sentences in sequence — "You get the keys. The seller gets paid. Everyone's happy." [^services-escrow] — a rhythm PCT uses for reassurance moments.
- Service descriptions trend medium-length and informational; calls-to-action and reassurance moments trend short.

### 4.5 Formality
- **Professional but not stiff.** No "thee/thou"-equivalent legal phrasing on consumer pages. The privacy policy, glossary, and FinCEN pages reach for more formal phrasing when accuracy matters, but PCT explicitly markets the FinCEN FAQ as "answered in plain English" [^fincen-faq] and the glossary as "explained in plain language" [^glossary].
- **"Plain English" / "plain language" is a stated value, not just a tone choice** — PCT names it twice as a deliverable [^fincen-faq] [^glossary].

### 4.6 Jargon: explained vs. assumed
- **Explained for consumers:** "What is title?" and "What is title insurance?" appear as plain-English FAQ headers on `/services/title` [^services-title]. The glossary defines 5+ basic terms (Abstract of Title, Acceleration Clause, Acknowledgment, ARM, Affidavit) in one-sentence form, then disclaims "not intended to be complete legal definitions" [^glossary].
- **Assumed for specialists:** BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information), AML (Anti-Money Laundering), and "trustee sales" are used without unpacking on the FinCEN overview [^fincen]. TSG-specific terms (REO, deed-in-lieu, eRecording) appear without definition on `/services/tsg` [^services-tsg].
- **The audience determines the vocabulary** — this is a consistent pattern.

### 4.7 Emotional / trust vocabulary
**Recurring words observed across pages:** trust, trusted, confidence, clarity, protect, protection, partner, reliable, experienced, dedicated, peace of mind (implied), neutral (re: escrow), expert/expertise [^homepage] [^about] [^services-title] [^services-escrow] [^services-commercial].

**Not observed:** "guaranteed," "best in the business," "premier," "leading," "fastest" — see §8.

## Section 8: Hard Rules — What PCT Doesn't Do

These are conspicuous absences from the public site. They are *evidence-based negatives* — if a future content piece breaks one of these, it would stand out as off-brand.

### 8.1 Vocabulary PCT avoids
Across 23 scanned pages, the following terms **did not appear** in any of the extracts:
- "Best in the industry," "premier," "leading," "#1," "top-rated"
- "Cheapest," "lowest-cost," "discount," "deal"
- "Guaranteed" / "100% guaranteed" (in a marketing-promise sense)
- "Revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing"
- "Hurry," "limited time," "today only," "act now," "while supplies last"
- "Powered by [competitor]" or any competitor name

### 8.2 Topics PCT doesn't cover on the public site
- **No competitor comparisons** of any kind. Other title companies are not named.
- **No political or social-issue content.** California Prop discussions are limited to property-tax mechanics (Prop 19, etc.), not the politics of them.
- **No opinion editorial.** Everything is explanatory or transactional.
- **No press releases or news of the firm.** No "PCT was named..." or "PCT acquired..." content surfaced.
- **No customer success stories or testimonials.** Surprising for a B2B service company — flagged in §10.
- **No leadership bios or team pages.** The About page references "45+ combined years experience" but no individuals [^about]. The only named individuals encountered were Analleli Ayala (1031 Exchange Specialist on `/services/1031-exchange`) [^services-1031] and TESSA™ (an AI).

### 8.3 Tones PCT doesn't use
- **No humor / no jokes.** Light moments exist (the "Everyone's happy" line on the escrow page) but are reassurance, not comedy.
- **No urgency marketing.** No countdown timers, no "act before X" framing — even the FinCEN page, which has a real regulatory deadline, frames it as *preparedness* not *panic*.
- **No fear-based copy** beyond the legitimate forgery / fraud / undisclosed-heir framing on `/services/title`, which is the standard category-of-coverage description, not a scare tactic.
- **No corporate / press-release voice** ("Pacific Coast Title Company is pleased to announce...") — that register doesn't appear on any scanned page.

### 8.4 Things PCT does **not** claim
- Specific turnaround times (e.g., "24-hour title search") — no SLAs are published [^services-title] [^services-tsg]
- Specific pricing or rate floors — rates are always behind a calculator or quote request
- Specific underwriter names — kept as "major national title insurers with A+ financial ratings" [^about]
- Specific customer counts beyond the round "100K+ transactions" claim [^about]
